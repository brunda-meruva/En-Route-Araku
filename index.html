Escape Life to Discover the Hidden Beauty of Araku Valley

Araku Valley, often called the "Ooty of Andhra Pradesh," is a scenic, eco-friendly hill station 115 km from Visakhapatnam in the Eastern Ghats. Renowned for its lush coffee plantations, misty valleys, and rich tribal culture, it offers a tranquil escape with key attractions like the 50-million-year-old Borra Caves and panoramic train journeys

Best Places To Visit

Discover the most beautiful destinations in Araku Valley

Borra Caves

Overview

These ancient Borra Caves are a geological marvel featuring stunning stalactite and stalagmite formations. Deep within the Ananthagiri Hills, the naturally lit limestone structures create a mystical atmosphere, making it a must-visit for adventure seekers and photography enthusiasts exploring the valley.

Plan Visit

Katiki Waterfalls

Overview

Reached via a thrilling jeep ride and a short trek, Katiki Waterfalls offers a refreshing escape into nature. The 50-foot cascade is surrounded by lush greenery, providing a perfect spot for travelers to relax and enjoy the famous local bamboo chicken.

Plan Visit

Coffee Plantations

Overview

The Araku Coffee Plantations blanket the hills in vibrant green, offering a fragrant and serene walking experience. Tourists can learn about organic farming practices and enjoy a fresh cup of world-class Arabica coffee while soaking in the panoramic views of the valley.

Plan Visit

Wooden Bridge

Overview

Tucked inside a coffee estate, the Aranya Wooden Bridge provides an enchanting canopy-level walk through the forest. It is a favorite spot for couples and photographers, offering a unique perspective of the towering trees, pepper vines, and the tranquil mountain landscape.

Plan Visit

Padmapuram Gardens

Overview

Originally a wartime kitchen garden, Padmapuram Gardens is now a popular botanical park. Families enjoy the toy train rides and the chance to stay in "hanging cottages," which are unique tree-top huts nestled among exotic flowers and rare plant species.

Plan Visit

Tribal Museum

Overview

The Araku Tribal Museum provides a deep dive into the indigenous culture of the Eastern Ghats. Through lifelike dioramas, traditional art, and handicrafts, tourists gain insight into the local lifestyle, making it an essential stop for those interested in regional history.

Plan Visit

Chocolate Factory

Overview

A sweet addition to any itinerary, the Araku Chocolate Factory showcases the bean-to-bar process. Visitors can watch how artisanal chocolates are made using local ingredients and purchase unique, handmade treats that often blend the rich flavors of regional cocoa and coffee.

Plan Visit

Strawberry Farms

Overview

During the winter season, the Araku Strawberry Farms offer a delightful "pick-and-eat" experience. Tourists can walk through the rows of red berries, learn about hill-station agriculture, and buy fresh, organic produce directly from the local farmers in a scenic setting.

Plan Visit

Popular Destinations

Don't just stop at Araku, without exploring these popular nearby destinations

Visakhapatnam

The City Of Destiny

Visakhapatnam, commonly referred to as Vizag, is an exceptional coastal gem with stunning beaches, lush hillsides, clear skies and vibrant culture. Golden beaches provide stunning views and cool waters - the ideal getaway!

Lambasingi

Andhra's Kashmir

Lambasingi Hills, commonly dubbed the "Kashmir of Andhra Pradesh," is famous for its exquisite beauty at dawn, especially during sunrise when misty fog fills the landscape to form an exquisite scene of peace and natural wonder.

Vanjangi

Meghala Samudhram

Vanjangi Hills, an exquisite gem of nature, mesmerizes visitors with its lush landscapes, and timelessness beauty. Morning Meghala Samudram radiates mysticism; here clouds kiss the horizon while serenity reigns supreme.

Book Now

Discover the age-old traditions and cultural essence of the Tribes of Araku Valley

Araku’s tribal culture is a vibrant tapestry of 19 indigenous groups living in harmony with the Eastern Ghats. Their identity thrives through the rhythmic Dhimsa dance, sustainable forest-based living, and a deep spiritual bond with nature. This heritage is beautifully expressed in their colorful attire, hand-woven crafts, and a resilient community spirit that defines the valley’s soul.
